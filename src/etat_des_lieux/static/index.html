The bottleneck

Report production should not depend on manual retyping

Many firms still produce property condition reports by recording observations on-site, then sending the audio to an internal admin team, freelancer, or offshore provider to create the final report. This creates delays, corrections, inconsistent wording, and dependency on external availability.

Exception-based review

Review the exceptions, not the whole report

The goal is not to force your team to reread every line. Voice2Doc highlights uncertain or risky items, such as unclear audio, ambiguous damage descriptions, missing information, low-confidence sections, or unusual observations.

FAQ

Common questions

Does this fully replace human review?

No. Voice2Doc generates a structured draft and highlights uncertain items. A human reviewer validates the final report.

Do we need to change our report template?

No. The system is adapted to your existing template.

What if the audio is unclear?

Unclear passages are flagged. The reviewer can jump back to the relevant transcript or audio section.

Can it support French and Dutch?

The workflow is designed for multilingual Belgian inspection processes. Language support is configured during onboarding.

Can we test without sharing sensitive client data?

Yes. The first test can use an anonymized past recording and report.

What file do we receive?

The final output can be exported as an editable Word document and, if needed, as PDF.
